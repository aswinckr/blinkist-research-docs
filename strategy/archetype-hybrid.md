### 6.2 Critical Risk: Message Clarity

**Definition:** "AI + Human" is harder to explain than pure AI or pure human positioning.

**Evidence:**
- Market messaging typically requires simple, singular positioning
- "Both" can feel like fence-sitting rather than differentiation

**Mitigations:**
1. **Lead with the 91% activation story** â€” "AI that's 91% more effective with human moments"
2. **Hero metric** â€” Use activation rate as differentiating proof point
3. **Simple tier names** â€” Self-Serve, Guided, Coached, Certified
4. **Compare to competitors** â€” "They offer AI OR human. We offer AI + Human."

### 6.3 Critical Risk: Operational Complexity

**Definition:** Running two delivery models (AI + Human) is harder than running one.

**Evidence:**
- [E-WS-04]: Workshop materials acknowledge operational challenge
- Different skill sets, metrics, and processes required

**Mitigations:**
1. **Start with existing assets** â€” Coaching and Live Sessions already exist
2. **Technology-enable everything** â€” Reduce admin burden on human elements
3. **Standardize programs** â€” Repeatable frameworks, not custom every time
4. **Phase rollout** â€” Prove one tier before building next
5. **Partner where needed** â€” External coach network for scale
