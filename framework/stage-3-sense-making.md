## Stage Definition

> **Core Question:** "Do I truly understand this?"

Sense-Making is the comprehension stage where learners transform raw information into genuine understanding. This is where learning moves beyond surface-level exposure to deep knowledge integration â€” connecting new concepts to existing mental models and developing the ability to explain, question, and apply.

### What Success Looks Like

| Dimension | Failed Sense-Making | Successful Sense-Making |
|-----------|---------------------|-------------------------|
| **Depth** | Surface recall, can repeat facts | Deep understanding, can explain why |
| **Connection** | Isolated information, standalone facts | Integrated with existing knowledge |
| **Questioning** | Accepts everything presented | Asks "but what about...?" |
| **Translation** | Uses source vocabulary only | Translates to own context and language |
| **Retention** | Forgets within hours/days | Remembers weeks/months later |
| **Transfer** | Cannot apply to new situations | Adapts principles to novel contexts |

## The Passive Consumption Trap

> **"Reading/listening â‰  understanding. Most learners confuse exposure with comprehension. They nod along without ever stress-testing their understanding."** [E-LDT-04]

### The Core Problem

The human brain is designed to conserve cognitive energy. Given a choice, learners will:
- Listen passively rather than engage actively
- Accept information rather than question it
- Move on rather than process deeply
- Feel confident without verified comprehension

**Evidence:** [E-LDT-04]
> "Surface-level engagement â€” passive, copy-paste without critical thinking" is a key failure mode of AI-assisted learning.

### The Blinkist Paradox

Blinkist's core value proposition creates a structural sense-making challenge:

| Blinkist Strength | Sense-Making Implication |
|-------------------|-------------------------|
| **Summaries** | Someone else did the sense-making |
| **15-minute format** | Less time for deep processing |
| **Audio-first** | Passive consumption is easy |
| **Efficient delivery** | Quick exposure, quick forgetting |

**The Tension:** Blinkist solves the problem of "too much content, too little time" but may inadvertently enable the very passive consumption that undermines learning.

### Consumer vs. Enterprise Impact

| Context | Passive Consumption Risk | Stakes |
|---------|-------------------------|--------|
