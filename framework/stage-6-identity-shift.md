## The Identity Shift Threshold

### What Makes It Different

Identity Shift is qualitatively different from all previous stages:

| Stage 1-5 | Stage 6 |
|-----------|---------|
| Adding knowledge/skills | Redefining self-concept |
| External behaviors | Internal beliefs |
| Conscious application | Unconscious embodiment |
| "I can do X" | "I am someone who does X" |
| Reversible with decay | Durable through challenges |
| Individual change | Often social/relational |

### The Transformation Markers

Evidence suggests identity shift occurs through specific markers:

**1. Narrative Change**
> The learner's story about themselves changes. "I used to be someone who struggled with public speaking. Now I'm the person who facilitates meetings."

**2. Emotional Investment**
> Strong emotional connection to the capability. Not just skill, but pride, meaning, purpose. [E-LDT-18]

**3. Teaching Disposition**
> Desire and ability to help others develop. "If you can teach it, you own it" â€” teaching cements identity.

**4. Advocacy Behavior**
> Proactively recommending and championing the approach. Not just using, but spreading.

**5. Resilience Under Pressure**
> The change survives stress. Someone who truly "became a leader" doesn't revert when things get hard.
