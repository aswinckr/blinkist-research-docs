## Executive Summary

The Breadth vs. Depth tension represents one of the most consequential strategic choices Blinkist faces. This analysis explores the trade-off between two fundamentally different approaches to corporate learning:

| Dimension | Breadth Strategy | Depth Strategy |
|-----------|------------------|----------------|
| **Core question** | "How do we reach everyone?" | "How do we transform anyone?" |
| **Success metric** | Users touched | Lives changed |
| **Unit economics** | Low cost per learner | High value per learner |
| **Competitive moat** | Scale advantages | Outcome differentiation |
| **AI role** | Core delivery mechanism | Supporting tool |
| **Human role** | Optional enhancement | Essential ingredient |

**Strategic Paradox:** Blinkist's B2C heritage (40M users) suggests breadth, but its strongest B2B evidence (Perlego +41 NPS [Q-003]) comes from depth. The company currently straddles both without fully committing to either.

**What This Looks Like:**
- LinkedIn Learning, Udemy Business, Go1 model
- Maximize library size, language coverage, integrations
- AI handles all personalization and discovery
- Coaching eliminated or spun off as separate business

## The Blinkist Paradox

### B2C Heritage = Breadth DNA

Blinkist's 40M B2C user base was built on breadth:
- 15-minute summaries (quick, accessible)
- Audio format (passive consumption)
- Self-directed discovery
- Low commitment per title
- Individual subscription model

### B2B Evidence = Depth Value

Blinkist's strongest B2B evidence comes from depth:
- Perlego +41 NPS from Coaching (not content)
- 91% spike from Live Sessions (human moments)
- HAYS quote: "employees' enthusiasm" (culture change)
- Amazon quote: mentioning learning culture (transformation)

### The Straddling Problem

Current positioning attempts to serve both:
- **Pricing:** Per-seat (breadth model)
- **Product:** 3 modes "equal" (hybrid claim)
- **Sales narrative:** Leads with content (breadth), supports with coaching (depth)
- **Evidence cited:** Mix of breadth metrics (adoption) and depth outcomes (NPS)

**Risk:** Straddling often fails because:
1. Message confusion â€” Buyers don't know what Blinkist is
2. Resource splitting â€” Neither mode gets full investment
3. Sales friction â€” Different buyers, different motions
4. Competitive vulnerability â€” Pure plays attack from both sides

## Strategic Options

### Option A: Double Down on Breadth

**Execute if:** You believe scale advantages can be achieved before commoditization eliminates margins.

**Actions:**
1. Kill or spin off Coaching â€” it's a distraction
2. Maximize content library, languages, integrations
3. Compete on price per seat at scale
4. Lead with adoption/engagement metrics
5. Target procurement buyers, not L&D leaders
6. Accept: No outcome claims possible

**Evidence alignment:** Uses [STAT-005], [STAT-001], abandons [Q-003]

### Option B: Pivot to Depth

**Execute if:** You believe the market is shifting to outcome-based buying and human coaching is defensible.

**Actions:**
1. Reposition Coaching as core product, not add-on
2. Content becomes "preparation" for human programs
3. Price on outcomes, not seats
4. Lead with Perlego case study
5. Target L&D leaders and CHROs
6. Accept: Smaller but more valuable market

**Evidence alignment:** Uses [Q-003], [E-LDT-02], partially abandons [STAT-001]

### Option C: Strategic Segmentation

**Execute if:** You believe both buyer types exist and can be served without operational chaos.

**Actions:**
1. Create clear product tiers (Self-Serve â†’ Guided â†’ Coached)
2. Price each tier appropriately (breadth pricing â†’ depth pricing)
3. Build upsell path from breadth customers to depth programs
4. Different sales motions for different buyers
5. Measure different metrics for different segments
6. Accept: Operational complexity, message complexity

**Evidence alignment:** Uses all evidence, but requires clear segmentation story

### Option D: Depth-Led Breadth

**Execute if:** You believe depth creates the evidence that sells breadth.

**Actions:**
1. Lead sales conversations with depth outcomes (Perlego)
2. Use depth evidence to justify breadth pricing premium
3. Coaching becomes the "proof" that justifies per-seat investment
4. Every enterprise customer gets some depth moment (even if small)
5. Breadth access follows from depth relationship
6. Accept: More complex sales, requires depth capacity

**Evidence alignment:** [Q-003] opens doors, [STAT-005] expands relationships
