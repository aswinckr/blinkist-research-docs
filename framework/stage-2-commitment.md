## Stage Definition

> **Core Question:** "Why should I bother?"

Commitment is the motivation stage where learners decide to invest time, attention, and cognitive energy in learning something. This is the critical bridge between awareness (knowing content exists) and action (actually engaging deeply).

### What Success Looks Like

| Dimension | Failed Commitment | Successful Commitment |
|-----------|------------------|----------------------|
| **Motivation Source** | Compliance, external mandate | Intrinsic curiosity, personal relevance |
| **Investment Quality** | Minimum viable effort | Deliberate practice mindset |
| **Follow-Through** | Starts but doesn't finish | Completes and seeks more |
| **Emotional State** | Obligation, dread | Anticipation, curiosity |
| **Sustainability** | Drops off after initial push | Sustained engagement over time |
| **Ownership** | "They made me do this" | "I chose this for my growth" |
