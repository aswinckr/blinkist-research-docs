## Executive Summary

The CHRO/VP People is the **economic buyer** for enterprise learning platforms — they approve the budget, assess strategic fit, and evaluate risk. Unlike the L&D Director (operational champion), the CHRO thinks in terms of organizational outcomes: talent retention, leadership pipeline, productivity, and workforce agility. In 2026, they're navigating the tension between AI-driven efficiency and the human skills that drive competitive advantage.

**Key Insight:** 51% of CHROs cite **leadership and manager development** as their top 2025 priority, making Blinkist's leadership-focused content library highly relevant to this buyer. [BP-CHRO-001]

## 5. Decision-Making Unit (DMU) Position

### Where CHRO Sits in the Buying Process

| Role | CHRO's Position | L&D Director's Position |
|------|-----------------|-------------------------|
| **Initiator** | Sometimes (strategic mandate) | Usually (operational need) |
| **Influencer** | Always | Always |
| **Decider** | Final approval | Recommendation |
| **Buyer** | Budget owner | Request submitter |
| **User** | Indirect (dashboards, reports) | Direct (admin, analytics) |
| **Gatekeeper** | Risk assessment | Vendor evaluation |

### What CHRO Needs from L&D Director

- Clear business case with ROI projection
- Alignment with strategic priorities (leadership, AI, skills)
- Risk assessment (vendor stability, data security, implementation timeline)
- Reference customers (ideally in same industry/scale)
- Quick wins / early proof points from pilot

### What CHRO Asks That L&D Director Can't Answer

| Question | Why It's Hard | Blinkist Opportunity |
|----------|---------------|---------------------|
| "What's the ROI in $ terms?" | L&D metrics don't translate to $ | Provide case studies with financial impact |
| "How does this affect productivity?" | Hard to isolate learning's contribution | Develop productivity proxy metrics |
| "What's the competitive risk if we don't do this?" | L&D doesn't typically frame competitively | Frame as talent retention/acquisition edge |
| "Is this vendor stable?" | L&D focused on product, not company | Emphasize funding, customer base, longevity |

## 7. Objections and Responses

### Common CHRO Objections

| Objection | Root Concern | Recommended Response |
|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| "We already have an LMS" | Switching cost, sunk cost | Position as complement ("engagement layer"), not replacement |
| "Can you prove ROI?" | Accountability to board | Provide case studies with metrics; acknowledge measurement gap, offer to co-develop |
| "Is this just another AI gimmick?" | Vendor noise, differentiation | Lead with human coaching differentiator, downplay AI positioning |
| "We need enterprise security" | Risk, compliance | Highlight SOC 2, GDPR compliance, customer references |
| "Budget is tight" | Prioritization | Frame as productivity investment, not training expense; show TCO advantage |
| "We tried microlearning before" | Past failure experience | Differentiate on content quality (curated, not UGC), engagement data |

### What NOT to Say to a CHRO

- ❌ "Our AI is cutting-edge" → Every vendor says this
- ❌ "We have lots of content" → They're drowning in content
- ❌ Technical details → Delegate to L&D Director
- ❌ "We're disrupting learning" → Sounds risky, unproven
- ❌ Overselling → CHROs detect BS quickly

### What WORKS with CHROs

- ✅ "This directly supports your leadership development priority"
- ✅ "Here's a comparable CHRO who saw X results"
- ✅ "Implementation takes weeks, not months"
- ✅ "We're the engagement layer — complements your existing LMS"
- ✅ "Our pricing is transparent and volume-friendly"

## 10. Key Takeaways for Blinkist

### Strategic Recommendations

1. **Lead with leadership development alignment** — 51% CHRO priority directly maps to Blinkist's core library [BP-CHRO-001]

2. **Develop CHRO-specific case studies** — Need peer validation at executive level, not just L&D testimonials

3. **Create ROI calculator** — CHROs need to build internal business case; help them quantify value

4. **Address the 72% methodology gap** — CRITICAL for CHRO credibility; they will ask "how do you measure this?"

5. **Position as "engagement layer"** — Complements LMS, reduces switching cost concern

6. **Human coaching is the differentiator** — CHROs are skeptical of AI claims; human element is defensible

7. **Enterprise credibility** — Need Fortune 500 logos, analyst mentions, compliance certifications

8. **Scenario planning opportunity** — CHROs are leaning into scenario planning; could position around workforce agility
