## Executive Summary

The L&D Director is the primary decision-maker for enterprise learning platforms. In 2026, they're shifting from simply delivering training to building "capability ecosystems" powered by AI, data-driven decision-making, and fostering a culture of continuous, personalized learning.

### Decision Criteria Details

**AI-Powered Personalization** [BP-LD-019]
- Recommend content based on skill gaps and roles
- Tailor learning paths to individual needs
- Reduce time-to-find relevant content

**Data Analytics & Reporting** [BP-LD-020]
- Actionable insights, not just completion data
- Skill tracking and gap analysis
- Drop-off points, engagement levels
- Business impact correlation

**Integration Capabilities** [BP-LD-021]
- Seamless with Slack, MS Teams, HRIS
- SSO (Single Sign-On)
- API access for custom integrations
- Existing LMS compatibility

**User Experience** [BP-LD-022]
- Modern, mobile-friendly interface
- Consumer-grade UX ("Netflix for learning")
- Minimal friction for learners
- Fast time-to-value

## 5. Buying Journey & Behavior

### Buying Stages

| Stage | Activities | Timeline | Touchpoints |
|-------|------------|----------|-------------|
| **Problem Recognition** | Engagement drops, skill gap identified, leadership mandate | -6 to -3 months | Internal complaints, business metrics |
| **Solution Exploration** | Research vendors, read reports, peer recommendations | -3 to -2 months | G2, Capterra, LinkedIn, conferences |
| **Requirements Definition** | RFP creation, stakeholder alignment, budget approval | -2 to -1 month | Internal meetings, procurement |
| **Evaluation** | Demos, pilots, reference calls | -1 month to 0 | Vendor presentations, free trials |
| **Decision** | Stakeholder consensus, contract negotiation | 0 to +1 month | Procurement, legal |
| **Implementation** | Onboarding, rollout, change management | +1 to +3 months | Vendor support, internal comms |

### 2026 Buying Behavior Patterns

| Pattern | Description | Evidence |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| **"Design Backwards"** | Start with business outcomes, define KPIs, THEN look for technology | [BP-LD-023] |
| **Pilot Before Scaling** | Small group pilots to reduce risk before enterprise deployment | [BP-LD-024] |
| **Peer Validation** | Heavy reliance on peer recommendations, case studies, references | [BP-LD-025] |
| **Sense-making Focus** | Need help navigating noisy L&D tech market | [BP-LD-026] |

### Decision-Making Unit (DMU)

| Role | Influence | Primary Concerns |
|------|-----------|------------------|
| **L&D Director** (Champion) | HIGH — drives selection | Learning impact, UX, content quality |

### Messaging Recommendations

**Lead with:**
- Time-to-value (engagement in minutes, not months)
- Curation (antidote to content overload)

**Avoid:**
- Over-claiming on "flow of work" integration
- Competing on AI differentiation (commoditized)
- Analytics/skills intelligence comparisons

**Position as:**
- "The engagement layer" on top of LMS/LXP
- "Leadership microlearning" specialist
- "Curated knowledge, not content library"

## 10. Key Takeaways for Blinkist

1. **Engagement is #1 pain point** — Blinkist's 15-min format directly addresses this
2. **ROI proof is critical** — Current 72% claim needs methodology documentation
3. **Curation beats volume** — L&D Directors are drowning in content; Blinkist's curation is a differentiator
4. **Human coaching is the whitespace** — No competitor offers it; validates strategic direction
5. **Integration gaps** — Need to expand beyond ~10 integrations for enterprise buyers
6. **Skills intelligence is the new battlefield** — Blinkist should consider strategic response
7. **Position as engagement layer** — Complement LMS/LXP, don't compete head-on
