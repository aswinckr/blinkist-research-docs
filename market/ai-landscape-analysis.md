## Executive Summary

AI in Learning & Development has moved from hype to operational reality in 2025-2026. This analysis separates verified outcomes from marketing claims to guide Blinkist's strategic positioning.

|----------|------------|---------------|----------------------|
| AI-Powered Personalization | VERY HIGH | MODERATE | Works for path recommendations, not deep personalization |

| AI Analytics | MODERATE | MODERATE | Useful for patterns; attribution still challenging |
| Skills Intelligence | HIGH | EMERGING | Early but promising; data quality is the bottleneck |

## What the Research Says: AI's Learning Limitations

### From L&D Trends Report (159 pages analyzed)

**Finding 1: AI Makes Human-Led Learning MORE Valuable** [E-LDT-02]
> "As AI gets better at sharing and personalizing information, the value of traditional formats, especially training and even some workshops, starts to fade... Human-led learning needs to offer something AI can't: connection, collective sense-making, shared emotion, and identity shifts."

**Finding 2: AI Has Clear Limitations** [E-LDT-05]
Research-documented AI learning downsides:
- **Surface-level engagement:** Passive, copy-paste without critical thinking
- **Over-reliance:** Treating AI as single source of truth
- **Confident errors:** AI can be confidently wrong; novice learners vulnerable
- **Prompting gaps:** Most learners don't know how to ask good questions

**Finding 3: The Most Important "AI Skills" Are Human Skills** [E-LDT-06]
Effective AI use requires fundamentally human capabilities:
- Critical thinking (evaluating AI outputs)
- Creativity (using AI as collaborator)
- Delegation (knowing what to offload)
- Clear expectations and feedback
- Strategic decision-making
