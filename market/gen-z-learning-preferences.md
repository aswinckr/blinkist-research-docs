## Executive Summary

Gen Z (born 1997-2012) now represents **50 million workers** in the U.S. workforce alone and is projected to comprise **30% of the total workforce by 2030**. This generation's learning preferences differ dramatically from predecessors — they're digital natives who paradoxically crave human connection, demand personalized development, and expect AI-enhanced training while recognizing its limitations.

**Key implications for Blinkist:** Gen Z's preferences validate the hybrid Human + AI strategy. They want AI efficiency but also mentorship, video-based learning, microlearning, and real human guidance — exactly what Blinkist can offer.

## Key Takeaways for Blinkist Strategy

1. **Gen Z demands development** — 96% say it's important; this is a retention lever
2. **AI is expected but not sufficient** — 68% use AI tools but 63% want soft skills training too
3. **Human connection remains critical** — 49% prefer mentorship, 57% prefer in-person work
4. **Video/audio format wins** — 77% prefer video-based content; Blinkist's audio aligns
5. **Microlearning fits their habits** — 54% spend 4+ hours on social media daily
6. **The soft skills gap is real** — Gen Z ranks interpersonal skills 4th of 5; biggest need

**Strategic Summary:** Gen Z validates Blinkist's hybrid approach. They want AI efficiency with human depth — exactly the positioning Blinkist should own.
