## Executive Summary

The enterprise L&D platform buyer journey is **a complex, often non-linear process involving multiple stakeholders, high-stakes decision-making, and significant research**. For enterprise learning solutions, the journey is characterized by:

- **Long sales cycles:** 9-18 months for enterprise (vs. 3-6 months mid-market, 1-3 months SMB)
- **Multiple stakeholders:** IT, HR/L&D, Finance, Executives all involved
- **Heavy research focus:** ROI, scalability, integration, security compliance
- **Post-purchase extension:** Implementation, retention, and advocacy matter as much as the sale

[BJ-001: Google AI Overview, Feb 2026]

## 2. Stage 1: Awareness (Problem Identification)

### Overview

The organization recognizes a critical learning or development gap. The buyer is **not yet looking for a specific product**, but rather trying to understand, name, and frame their problem.

### Triggers

| Trigger | Frequency | Blinkist Relevance |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| Skill gaps / upskilling needs | Very High | ✅ Core strength |
| High employee turnover | High | ✅ Engagement benefit |
| Compliance risks | High | ⚠️ Not primary focus |
| Digital transformation pressure | High | ✅ Modern L&D positioning |
| Leadership pipeline gaps | High | ✅ Leadership content library |
| Low training engagement | Medium | ✅ 15-min format solves this |

[BJ-004: Google AI Overview, Feb 2026]

### Buyer Behavior

- Searching for: "how to improve employee engagement," "compliance training solutions," "scaling training for remote teams"
- Consuming: Thought leadership articles, webinars, industry reports (Gartner/Forrester), whitepapers
- Not yet vendor-focused

[BJ-005: Google AI Overview, Feb 2026]

### Key Stakeholders

| Role | Activity | Influence |
|------|----------|-----------|
| L&D Director | Identifies problem, researches solutions | Champion |
| HR Business Partner | Surfaces symptoms (turnover, skills gaps) | Influencer |
| Line Managers | Report training effectiveness issues | Problem source |
| Employees | Express learning frustrations | End users |

### Content That Works

| Content Type | Purpose | Blinkist Action |
|--------------|---------|-----------------|
| Industry reports | Frame the problem | Create L&D trends digest |
| Benchmark studies | Quantify the gap | Publish engagement benchmarks |
| Thought leadership | Build credibility | VP/CEO guest posts |
| Webinars | Educate without selling | "Future of L&D" series |

### Blinkist Recommendations

1. **Create problem-framing content:** "The 5 Signs Your L&D Program Isn't Working"
2. **Publish engagement benchmarks:** Use the 96% completion rate and 3x engagement data
3. **Build SEO presence:** Target "how to improve employee learning engagement" queries
4. **Develop L&D trends newsletter:** Position as industry thought leader

## 3. Stage 2: Evaluation (Consideration & Research)

### Overview

The buyer has defined their problem and is **actively researching, evaluating, and comparing potential solutions**. They are looking for specific functionality and proof of value.

### Duration

- **Enterprise:** 2-4 months (can extend to 6+ months for complex deals)
- **Mid-market:** 4-8 weeks
- **SMB:** 1-2 weeks

[BJ-007: Aexus, Nov 2025]

[BJ-016: COMP-PM-024, competitor analysis]

### Implementation Success Factors

| Factor | Impact | Blinkist Position |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| SSO integration | High | ✅ Supported |
| Content customization | Medium | ⚠️ Limited (curated library) |
| Admin training | High | ✅ Simple admin dashboard |
| User onboarding | High | ✅ Intuitive consumer-grade UX |
| Success planning | High | Need to develop |
| Integration depth | Medium | ⚠️ Gap (limited integrations) |

### Launch Playbook Framework

| Week | Activity | Owner |
|------|----------|-------|
| 1 | Technical setup (SSO, integrations) | IT + Blinkist |
| 2 | Admin training, success planning | L&D + Blinkist CS |
| 3 | Pilot group launch (50-100 users) | L&D |
| 4 | Full rollout, marketing campaign | L&D + Internal Comms |

### Early Warning Signs (Churn Indicators)

| Indicator | Risk Level | Action |
|-----------|------------|--------|
| <20% login rate in first 30 days | HIGH | Proactive outreach |
| Admin hasn't logged in for 14+ days | HIGH | CS intervention |
| No content completion in first 7 days | MEDIUM | User activation campaign |
| Negative user feedback | MEDIUM | Product/CS escalation |

### Blinkist Recommendations

1. **Develop success planning framework:** Goal-setting with L&D Director at launch
2. **Create launch marketing kit:** Email templates, Slack announcements, posters
3. **Implement early warning system:** Usage monitoring for CS intervention
4. **Build 30/60/90 day check-in cadence:** Proactive relationship management
5. **Offer implementation certification:** Train admins as "Blinkist Champions"

## 15. Key Takeaways

### Critical Insights

1. **Sales cycle is a competitive advantage:** Blinkist's fast implementation (2-4 weeks) vs. competitors (3-12 months) can shorten sales cycle by 33%
2. **Multiple stakeholders require multi-threaded selling:** L&D Director is champion, but CHRO approves budget and IT/Security are gatekeepers
3. **Evaluation is the battleground:** Most deals are won or lost in the evaluation stage — need strong demos, case studies, and ROI proof
4. **Retention matters as much as acquisition:** Enterprise contracts are 1-3 years; expansion revenue and renewals are critical
5. **Content gaps limit awareness:** Need industry reports, benchmarks, and thought leadership to get on consideration lists
